Where this started

Hey {{ subscriber.first_name | strip | default: "there" }},

I spent fifteen years as a teacher. I was good at it. And somewhere in the last few of those years, I started noticing something that genuinely worried me.

The kids were getting better at school and worse at life.

They could fill in a worksheet but freeze on a phone call. Ace the test, then fall apart when a plan went sideways. The things that actually matter once you grow up, handling money, solving a real problem, sticking with something hard, were quietly going missing.

So I did something a little reckless. I took a year off, unpaid, and traveled with my own two kids. No curriculum. We learned by doing: budgeting in foreign currencies, reading maps, talking to strangers, figuring it out when the plan fell apart.

That year changed everything. My kids came home more capable, more curious, more themselves. I never went back to the classroom. I built Anywhere Learning instead, so other families could have what that year gave us, without quitting a job or flying anywhere.

The guide you grabbed is a small piece of it. If you haven't opened it yet, pick one activity this week and watch what happens when your kid is the one in charge. That's where it starts.
