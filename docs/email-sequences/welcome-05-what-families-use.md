**Preview text:** For the days when you draw a blank (we all have them)

---

The hardest part of no-curriculum learning isn't the learning. It's the "what do we do today?" feeling.

Some days the ideas flow. Your kids are outside building something, asking questions, completely absorbed. You think, *we've got this.*

Other days you stare at your kids over breakfast and your mind goes blank. They're bored. You're tired. And the temptation to pull up a worksheet just to feel like something is happening? Very real.

I've been there. More often than I'd like to admit.

That's actually why I started creating activity packs. Not a curriculum - more like a box of sparks. Open-ended prompts that kick off real-world adventures and then get out of the way. You print them, hand them over, and let your kids run with it.

Low prep. No lesson plans. No "correct answers."

Here's what they actually look like:

- **A neighbourhood mapping challenge** where kids explore their surroundings, talk to people, and create their own illustrated map - deciding what matters enough to include.
- **A family cooking project** that starts with a budget, a trip to the store, and a meal your kids plan and make from scratch. (My two do this most nights now. They argue about spices. It's great.)
- **A nature observation journal prompt** that sends kids outside with a magnifying glass and a question - not to fill in blanks, but to notice things they've never noticed before.

Every activity is designed to work anywhere - your kitchen, your backyard, a park, a new city. Ages 6 to 14, with built-in flexibility so siblings at different stages can do them together.

They're the kind of thing I wish I'd had on those blank-brain mornings. Something I could hand my kids that would spark real engagement without me having to be the idea machine every single day.
