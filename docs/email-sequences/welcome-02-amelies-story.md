# Welcome Email 2 — Amelie's Story

**Sequence:** Welcome
**Number:** 2 of 6
**Send delay:** Day 2
**Subject line:** I used to be a teacher. Then I stopped.
**Preview text:** What I saw in the classroom that I couldn't unsee

---

I spent 15 years in a classroom watching kids ace tests and then struggle to count change at a store.

Kids who could recite facts about ecosystems but had never planted a seed. Kids who could solve equations but couldn't figure out how to split a restaurant bill. Something was off, and once I saw it, I couldn't unsee it.

When I became a mom, the feeling only got louder. I watched my own kids — Zach and Julia — start to lose the spark I knew they had. The questions slowed down. The curiosity got quieter. School was working *on* them, but it wasn't working *for* them.

So we made a big, terrifying decision. We left.

Not just the school — eventually, we left the country. We packed up our life in Quebec and started traveling. Florida first, then Panama, Costa Rica, and now El Salvador.

And here's what happened: my kids came alive.

In Costa Rica, they attended a place called Jungle Academy — an outdoor school with no walls, surrounded by jungle. Twenty-something kids from all over the world, learning together in the open air. I watched Julia light up in a way I hadn't seen in years. Zach started asking questions I didn't have answers to (the best kind).

Now, in El Salvador, my 12-year-old surfs in the morning and researches the Panama Canal in the afternoon — in French, for the family blog he writes. My 9-year-old builds elaborate sand creatures at the beach and then draws posters about hurricanes for her YouTube videos. Neither of them thinks of it as "school." It's just life.

I'm not telling you this to brag or to say you need to sell everything and travel. I'm telling you because it changed what I believe learning can look like. And most of it? You can do it from your own kitchen, your backyard, your neighbourhood.

That's what Anywhere Learning is about.

I'd love to hear your story, too. Hit reply and tell me — what does learning look like in your family right now?

xo, Amelie
